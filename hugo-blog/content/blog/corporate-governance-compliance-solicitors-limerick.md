## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can directors be personally liable for company debts in Ireland?

In most circumstances, limited liability protects directors from personal responsibility for company debts.

However, directors may become personally liable if they engage in:

* Reckless trading
* Fraudulent trading
* Serious breaches of fiduciary duties
* Failure to maintain proper company records

Seeking legal advice can help minimise these risks.

### What is corporate governance?

Corporate governance refers to the systems, policies, and procedures used to direct and manage a company.

It includes:

* Board structures
* Accountability frameworks
* Decision-making processes
* Risk management systems
* Shareholder relations

Strong governance promotes transparency, compliance, and business stability.

### Why does my business need a shareholder agreement?

A shareholder agreement provides clear rules regarding:

* Ownership rights
* Share transfers
* Dividend distributions
* Management responsibilities
* Dispute resolution

Without a properly drafted agreement, disagreements can become costly and disruptive.

### How often should a company board meet?

There is no fixed legal requirement specifying how often directors must meet.

However, directors must meet frequently enough to properly oversee company affairs and fulfil their legal duties.

The appropriate frequency depends on the size, complexity, and activities of the business.
