# Drafting Wills

## Preserving Your Life's Work and Protecting Your Family

Creating a will is one of the most important steps you can take to protect your family and preserve the assets you have worked hard to build.

A properly drafted will provides certainty, reduces the risk of disputes, and ensures that your wishes are respected after your death.

Without a valid will, your estate may be distributed according to the rules of intestacy, which may not reflect your intentions or adequately provide for your loved ones.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide clear, practical advice to help you create a legally robust estate plan that protects your family and your legacy.

## The Risks of Leaving Your Estate Unplanned

Many people delay making a will because it can feel uncomfortable to think about the future.

However, failing to plan can create significant difficulties for those left behind.

Common concerns include:

* Will my spouse and children be financially secure?
* Who will manage my affairs if I become incapacitated?
* How can I protect vulnerable family members?
* What will happen to my business interests?
* Could my estate become the subject of family disputes?

A carefully prepared will helps eliminate uncertainty and provides peace of mind for both you and your loved ones.

## The Strategic Advantage: Future Planning and Enduring Powers of Attorney

Effective estate planning extends beyond what happens after death.

It also involves preparing for circumstances where you may no longer be able to make decisions independently.

We provide guidance on:

### Enduring Powers of Attorney (EPA)

An Enduring Power of Attorney allows you to appoint a trusted individual to make decisions on your behalf should you lose mental capacity in the future.

### Decision Support Service (DSS) Matters

We assist clients in understanding and implementing appropriate legal arrangements under Ireland's decision-support framework.

These measures help protect your dignity, independence, and personal wishes while ensuring trusted individuals can act when necessary.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why do I need a will?

A will ensures that your assets are distributed according to your wishes and can help minimise disputes among family members after your death.

Without a valid will, your estate will be distributed according to intestacy rules.

### Can I update my will in the future?

Yes.

A will should be reviewed regularly, particularly after significant life events such as:

* Marriage
* Divorce
* Birth of children
* Acquisition of significant assets
* Changes in financial circumstances

### What is an Enduring Power of Attorney?

An Enduring Power of Attorney allows you to appoint someone you trust to manage certain affairs if you lose the capacity to make decisions independently in the future.

### Can I include business assets in my will?

Yes.

Business interests can form an important part of your estate plan.

Proper succession planning helps ensure continuity and reduces disruption for family members, business partners, and employees.
