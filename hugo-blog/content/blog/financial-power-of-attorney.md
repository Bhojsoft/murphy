# Authoritative Financial POA Solicitors

## Secure Your Wealth, Property, and Business Interests

Financial planning is not only about building wealth. It is also about ensuring that your affairs can continue to be managed if you become unavailable, incapacitated, or unable to make decisions for yourself.

Without appropriate legal authority in place, even close family members may be unable to access bank accounts, manage investments, complete property transactions, or make important financial decisions on your behalf.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we help individuals, families, and business owners establish legally robust financial powers of attorney that provide certainty, protection, and peace of mind.

## Why Financial Powers of Attorney Matter

Unexpected illness, injury, travel commitments, or cognitive decline can leave important financial matters unresolved.

Clients frequently ask:

* Who will manage my finances if I am unable to do so?
* How can my spouse access essential funds if accounts are in my sole name?
* What happens to my business if I become incapacitated?
* Can someone complete a property transaction on my behalf while I am abroad?
* How do I avoid expensive court applications in the future?

Without proper planning, family members may face significant delays, legal complications, and financial uncertainty.

A carefully drafted power of attorney helps ensure that trusted individuals can act when needed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I appoint someone to sell property on my behalf?

Yes.

A specific power of attorney can be created to allow a trusted individual to manage a property sale, execute legal documentation, and complete the transaction on your behalf.

### What is the difference between a general power of attorney and an Enduring Power of Attorney?

A general power of attorney is typically effective while you retain capacity and may be limited to specific circumstances.

An Enduring Power of Attorney is designed to continue operating if you lose the capacity to make decisions in the future, subject to legal requirements.

### Can my attorney use my money for their own benefit?

No.

An attorney must act in your best interests and comply with strict legal duties when managing your affairs.

### Can a power of attorney cover business interests?

Yes.

Where appropriate, powers of attorney can be structured to address business-related matters, including company administration, commercial transactions, and operational continuity.
