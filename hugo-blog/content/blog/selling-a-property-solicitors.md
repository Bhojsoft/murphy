- Will missing documents delay the transaction?
- Could planning issues affect the sale?

Our role is to identify potential issues early and keep your sale moving forward efficiently.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### When should I instruct a solicitor when selling a property?

Ideally, you should instruct a solicitor before placing the property on the market.

Early preparation allows title documents to be reviewed and potential issues to be identified before a buyer is found.

### How are solicitor fees for selling a property calculated?

Costs generally include:

- Professional legal fees
- Land Registry fees
- Search fees
- Administrative outlays

We provide clear information regarding anticipated costs at the beginning of the transaction.

### Can I sell a property if I cannot locate the title deeds?

In many cases, yes.

The appropriate solution depends on whether the property is mortgaged and whether the title is registered.

We can advise on the steps required to establish ownership and proceed with the sale.

### What happens to my mortgage when the property is sold?

Upon completion, any outstanding mortgage is normally redeemed from the sale proceeds before the remaining balance is transferred to you.

We coordinate directly with your lender to ensure this process is completed correctly.
