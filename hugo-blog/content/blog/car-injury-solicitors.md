## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I accept an early settlement offer from the other driver's insurance company?

In most cases, no.

Early settlement offers are often made before the full extent of your injuries is known.

Once a settlement is accepted, you generally cannot pursue additional compensation later if your condition worsens.

It is important to obtain legal advice before accepting any offer.

### What happens if the accident was partially my fault?

You may still be entitled to compensation.

Under Irish law, compensation can still be awarded where responsibility for the accident is shared. Any award may be adjusted to reflect your level of responsibility.

We carefully analyse the evidence to ensure fault is assessed fairly.

### How do I pay medical expenses while my claim is ongoing?

Medical costs are often paid personally or through private health insurance initially.

We carefully record all treatment expenses and seek to recover these costs as part of your compensation claim.

### If the accident happened in a company vehicle, will making a claim affect my job?

Generally, no.

If another driver caused the accident, your claim is against that driver or their insurer.

Where employment-related issues arise, Irish employment law provides important protections for employees pursuing legitimate legal claims.
