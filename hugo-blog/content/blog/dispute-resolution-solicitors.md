## Alternative Dispute Resolution: A Smarter Approach

Court proceedings are not always the most effective solution.

In many cases, Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) offers a faster, more private, and significantly more cost-effective route to resolving disputes.

Our dispute resolution solicitors regularly assist clients through:

### Mediation

A structured negotiation process where an independent mediator helps both parties reach a mutually acceptable agreement.

### Conciliation

A less formal process designed to encourage constructive dialogue and practical settlement solutions.

### Arbitration

A private dispute resolution process where an independent arbitrator makes a legally binding decision outside the court system.

### Negotiated Settlements

Direct solicitor-to-solicitor negotiations designed to achieve favourable outcomes while preserving important relationships and avoiding unnecessary legal costs.

ADR frequently allows clients to retain greater control over the outcome while reducing stress, delay, and expense.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)?

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) refers to methods of resolving disputes outside of the traditional court system, including mediation, conciliation, arbitration, and negotiated settlement discussions.

ADR is often faster, less expensive, and more confidential than litigation.

### Is mediation legally binding?

The mediation process itself is not automatically legally binding.

However, any settlement agreement reached through mediation can usually be formalised into a legally binding agreement.

### When should I consider litigation?

Litigation may become necessary when negotiations, mediation, or other forms of ADR fail to resolve the dispute or where urgent legal intervention is required to protect your rights.

We will advise you if court proceedings become the most appropriate option.

### How long does dispute resolution take?

The timeframe depends on the complexity of the dispute and the resolution method used.

Some disputes can be resolved within weeks through negotiation or mediation, while more complex litigation may take considerably longer.
