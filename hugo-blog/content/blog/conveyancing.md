## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the conveyancing process take in Ireland?

Most residential transactions complete within approximately six to eight weeks after a property becomes sale agreed.

However, timelines can vary depending on factors such as:

- Title complexity
- Mortgage approval timelines
- Property chain issues
- Additional investigations required

We work proactively to minimise delays wherever possible.

### What costs should I expect when buying a property?

In addition to the purchase price, buyers should budget for:

- Legal fees
- Stamp Duty
- Land Registry fees
- Search fees
- Registration costs

We provide a clear breakdown of anticipated expenses at the outset of the transaction.

### Is "Sale Agreed" legally binding?

No.

A property becoming sale agreed does not create a legally binding contract.

Either party may withdraw from the transaction until formal contracts are signed and the necessary contractual obligations are completed.

### Do I need a structural survey before buying a property?

Yes.

Under the principle of caveat emptor ("buyer beware"), purchasers are responsible for investigating the condition of a property.

An independent structural survey can identify issues such as:

- Dampness
- Structural movement
- Subsidence
- Roofing defects
- Water ingress

We strongly recommend obtaining a professional survey before signing contracts.
