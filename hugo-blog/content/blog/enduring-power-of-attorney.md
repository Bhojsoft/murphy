Planning for the future is one of the most important decisions you can make for yourself and your loved ones.

An Enduring Power of Attorney (EPA) allows you to decide in advance who will manage your affairs if you become unable to make decisions due to illness, injury, or loss of mental capacity.

Without proper planning, your family may face significant legal and administrative challenges during an already difficult time.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we help clients put clear, legally robust arrangements in place to protect their dignity, financial security, and independence.

## The Importance of Planning Ahead

Many people assume their spouse, children, or close relatives can automatically make decisions on their behalf if they lose capacity.

Unfortunately, this is not the case.

Families often find themselves asking:

* What happens if I suffer a serious illness or stroke?
* Who will manage my finances if I can no longer do so?
* How will my bills, investments, or business interests be handled?
* Can my family access my bank accounts to pay for my care?

Without an Enduring Power of Attorney, loved ones may need to apply through formal legal processes to obtain authority to act on your behalf.

Creating an EPA while you have full capacity gives you control over who will make decisions and how those decisions should be managed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is an Enduring Power of Attorney?

An Enduring Power of Attorney is a legal document that allows you to appoint someone you trust to make decisions on your behalf if you lose the capacity to do so in the future.

### Who should I appoint as my attorney?

Most people appoint a spouse, adult child, close relative, or trusted friend.

The most important consideration is choosing someone who is responsible, trustworthy, and capable of acting in your best interests.

### Can an EPA be created after someone loses capacity?

No.

An individual must have the mental capacity to understand and sign the EPA at the time it is created.

This is why early planning is essential.

### Does an EPA cover financial and personal matters?

An EPA can be structured to address financial affairs and other personal decision-making matters, depending on your circumstances and wishes.

We can advise on the most appropriate arrangements for your situation.
