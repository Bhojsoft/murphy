## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I make a claim if the driver responsible left the scene?

Yes.

Claims may still be available in circumstances involving hit-and-run or untraced drivers.

Specific procedures and notification requirements apply, and legal advice should be sought as soon as possible.

### Should I speak directly with the other driver's insurance company?

It is generally advisable to seek legal advice before providing detailed statements or accepting settlement offers.

Early statements may affect your claim if not carefully handled.

### Can I claim compensation as a passenger?

Yes.

Passengers injured in road traffic accidents are often entitled to pursue compensation where another party was responsible for the collision.

### How long do I have to make a road traffic accident claim?

In most cases, personal injury claims in Ireland must be initiated within two years of:

- The date of the accident, or
- The date you became aware of the injury

Seeking legal advice promptly helps preserve important evidence and protect your legal rights.
