## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do I need to bring my own documents?

Yes.

As Commissioner for Oaths, our role is to witness signatures, administer oaths, and certify documents.

You should bring the completed document with you.

If you require assistance drafting an affidavit, declaration, or other legal document, we can provide that as a separate legal service.

### What identification should I bring?

You must bring valid original photographic identification.

Acceptable identification generally includes:

- A valid passport
- A valid driving licence

Photocopies and expired identification documents cannot be accepted.

### Can you visit a hospital or nursing home?

Yes.

We regularly assist clients who cannot travel due to illness, mobility difficulties, or other personal circumstances.

Where appropriate, we can arrange visits to hospitals, nursing homes, care facilities, and private residences.

### What is the difference between an affidavit and a statutory declaration?

An affidavit is a written statement sworn under oath before a Commissioner for Oaths.

A statutory declaration is a formal written declaration made under statutory authority without taking a religious oath.

We can advise on the appropriate witnessing process once your documentation has been prepared.
