## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a Full Repairing and Insuring (FRI) lease?

An FRI lease is one of the most common forms of commercial lease in Ireland.

Under this arrangement, the tenant is generally responsible for maintaining and repairing the property and may also contribute towards insurance costs.

Because these obligations can be extensive, legal review before signing is strongly recommended.

### Do I have to pay VAT on a commercial property purchase?

The VAT treatment of commercial property transactions can be complex and depends on factors such as the age of the property and the nature of the transaction.

We work alongside your accountant and financial advisers to ensure all VAT implications are fully understood before completion.

### Can I terminate a commercial lease early?

This depends on the terms of the lease.

If a break clause has been negotiated into the agreement, early termination may be possible subject to certain conditions.

Without a break clause, terminating a lease can be considerably more difficult.

We advise clients on their options and negotiate appropriate flexibility wherever possible.

### Should I provide a personal guarantee for a commercial lease?

Landlords often request personal guarantees from company directors.

Providing a guarantee can expose personal assets to potential liability if the business is unable to meet its obligations.

Before agreeing to any guarantee, it is important to obtain independent legal advice and fully understand the risks involved.
