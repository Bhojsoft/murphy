Frequently Asked Questions

Is a Lasting Power of Attorney the same as an Enduring Power of Attorney?

In practical terms, they serve a similar purpose.

A Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) is the term commonly used in England and Wales.

In Ireland, the equivalent legal arrangement is known as an Enduring Power of Attorney (EPA).

We advise clients on the Irish legal framework and prepare the appropriate documentation required under Irish law.

What affects the cost of an Enduring Power of Attorney?

The cost will depend on factors such as:

  • The complexity of your affairs
  • The nature of your assets
  • Business interests
  • Registration requirements
  • Professional certification requirements

We provide clear information regarding costs during your consultation.

Can an EPA be created after someone develops severe dementia?

No.

An individual must have the mental capacity necessary to understand the nature and effect of the document when it is signed.

If capacity has already been lost, alternative legal arrangements may need to be considered.

Early planning is therefore strongly recommended.

Who should I appoint as my attorney?

Most people appoint someone they trust completely, such as:

  • A spouse or partner
  • An adult child
  • A close relative
  • A trusted friend

The most important consideration is choosing someone who will act responsibly and in accordance with your wishes.

We can advise on the various appointment options available and help determine the most appropriate arrangement for your circumstances.
