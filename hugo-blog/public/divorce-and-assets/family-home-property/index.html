Family Home & Property

Securing Your Most Valuable Asset in a Divorce

The family home is often the most valuable asset involved in a separation or divorce.

Beyond its financial value, it represents security, stability, family memories, and, for many parents, continuity for their children.

When relationships break down, questions surrounding ownership, occupation, mortgage responsibility, and future housing arrangements can quickly become complex.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide practical, strategic legal advice to help clients protect their interests while working towards fair and sustainable property arrangements.

The Emotional and Financial Impact of Property Disputes

Many clients come to us with concerns such as:

  • Who gets the family home?
  • Will I have to sell the property?
  • Can my spouse force a sale?
  • What happens if both names are on the mortgage?
  • Can I remain in the home with the children?
  • What if I want to buy out my spouse’s share?

These concerns are entirely understandable.

Property issues often represent the largest financial aspect of any separation and can have long-term consequences for both parties.

Our role is to provide clarity, protect your legal rights, and help secure a practical outcome that supports your future.

Understanding Property Rights During Divorce

Every family situation is unique.

Irish courts do not automatically divide assets equally.

Instead, a range of factors may be considered, including:

  • The needs of dependent children
  • Financial contributions
  • Future earning capacity
  • Housing requirements
  • Pension provision
  • Overall family circumstances

Because every case is different, obtaining tailored legal advice is essential before making decisions about property ownership or settlement proposals.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who gets the family home in a divorce?

There is no automatic rule.

The outcome depends on the specific circumstances of each family, including financial resources, housing needs, and the welfare of any dependent children.

Can my spouse force the sale of the house?

Not necessarily.

Whether a property is sold, transferred, or retained by one party depends on the overall settlement and legal circumstances.

Can I buy out my spouse’s share of the property?

In many cases, yes.

This often involves obtaining an updated valuation and arranging mortgage refinancing where necessary.

What happens if both names are on the mortgage?

Even if one person leaves the property, both parties may remain legally responsible to the lender unless formal arrangements are made.

Are investment properties included in a divorce settlement?

Yes.

All relevant assets may be considered when determining an appropriate financial settlement.
