Protecting Your Financial Independence and Your Family’s Future

The breakdown of a marriage is one of life’s most challenging experiences.

While the emotional impact can be overwhelming, the financial consequences often have long-term implications for your future, your children, and your financial security.

When significant assets, businesses, pensions, investments, or family property are involved, it is essential to obtain clear legal advice and experienced representation.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide practical, strategic guidance to help clients navigate complex financial matters while protecting their long-term interests.

The High-Stakes Reality of Separating a Life’s Work

For many individuals, divorce creates uncertainty about everything they have spent years building.

You may be asking:

  • Will I receive a fair share of the family assets?
  • How will the family home be dealt with?
  • What happens to pensions and retirement savings?
  • Are all financial assets being fully disclosed?
  • How can I protect my children’s future?

These concerns are entirely understandable.

A poorly structured financial settlement can affect your financial independence for decades. That is why careful planning and experienced legal guidance are critical from the outset.

We help remove uncertainty by providing clear advice, practical solutions, and strong legal representation throughout the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are assets divided during a divorce in Ireland?

There is no automatic formula for dividing assets.

The court considers a range of factors including:

  • The financial circumstances of both parties
  • Contributions made during the marriage
  • Future financial needs
  • The welfare of any children involved

The objective is to achieve a fair and equitable outcome.

What happens to pensions during a divorce?

Pensions may be considered part of the overall financial settlement.

In some cases, a Pension Adjustment Order may be made to allocate a portion of one spouse’s pension benefits to the other.

What if I believe my spouse is hiding assets?

If there are concerns regarding incomplete financial disclosure, we can take steps to investigate and obtain the necessary financial information.

This may include reviewing business interests, investment accounts, property ownership, and other financial assets.

Do I have to go to court to resolve financial matters?

Not always.

Many financial settlements can be resolved through negotiation, mediation, or solicitor-led discussions without the need for a full court hearing.

Where court proceedings become necessary, we provide strong legal representation throughout the process.
