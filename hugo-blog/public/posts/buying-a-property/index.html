Frequently Asked Questions

When should I hire a house purchase solicitor?

The ideal time is before you begin actively bidding on properties.

Once a property becomes sale agreed, estate agents will immediately request your solicitor’s details.

Having your solicitor instructed early helps prevent delays and demonstrates to the seller that you are a serious and prepared buyer.

Is my booking deposit refundable if the sale falls through?

Yes.

A booking deposit is generally paid subject to contract and does not create a legally binding obligation to purchase.

If legal issues arise during the transaction or mortgage approval is not obtained, the booking deposit is typically refundable.

What is a snag list for a new-build property?

A snag list is a detailed inspection report prepared by an independent engineer before completion.

It identifies:

  • Cosmetic defects
  • Incomplete works
  • Structural concerns
  • Finishing issues

We ensure appropriate contractual protections are in place so these matters can be addressed before final completion.

Do I need a structural survey for a second-hand property?

Yes.

Under the principle of caveat emptor (“buyer beware”), purchasers are responsible for investigating the physical condition of a property.

A structural survey can identify significant issues such as:

  • Dampness
  • Subsidence
  • Roof defects
  • Structural movement
  • Water ingress

We strongly recommend obtaining an independent engineer’s report before signing contracts.
