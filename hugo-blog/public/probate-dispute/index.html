Resolving Family Conflicts with Clarity and Gravitas

When a loved one passes away, the grieving process is difficult enough without the added strain of a legal dispute over their estate.

Probate disputes can create significant emotional stress, damage family relationships, and place valuable assets at risk.

Whether you are an executor defending a will, a beneficiary seeking answers, or a family member concerned about the administration of an estate, you need experienced legal guidance and clear advice.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide practical, strategic representation designed to protect your rights while working toward an efficient resolution.

The Emotional and Financial Toll of Probate Disputes

For many families, probate litigation is about far more than money.

Questions often arise such as:

  • Was my loved one influenced into changing their will?
  • Did they fully understand the document they signed?
  • Is the estate being administered properly?
  • Have assets been overlooked or mismanaged?
  • Am I receiving the inheritance I am legally entitled to?

These concerns can create significant anxiety during an already difficult time.

We provide straightforward legal advice that helps clients understand their position, assess their options, and move forward with confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can a will be challenged in Ireland?

Yes.

A will may be challenged in certain circumstances, including allegations of:

  • Undue influence
  • Lack of capacity
  • Fraud
  • Improper execution

Each case depends on its specific facts and evidence.

What is a Section 117 application?

A Section 117 application allows a child to ask the court to consider whether a parent failed in their moral duty to make proper provision for them through their estate.

Specific legal requirements and time limits apply.

Can an executor be removed?

In certain circumstances, the court may intervene where an executor is unable or unwilling to properly administer an estate.

Applications of this nature require careful legal assessment.

Are probate disputes always resolved in court?

No.

Many probate disputes are resolved through negotiation, mediation, or other forms of alternative dispute resolution without the need for a full court hearing.

How quickly should I seek legal advice?

Probate disputes often involve strict legal deadlines.

Seeking legal advice as early as possible helps preserve evidence and ensures your rights are protected.
