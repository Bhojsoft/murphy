Frequently Asked Questions

How is child maintenance calculated in Ireland?

There is no fixed statutory formula for calculating child maintenance.

Instead, the courts consider factors such as:

  • The financial needs of the child
  • The income and earning capacity of both parents
  • Educational expenses
  • Healthcare costs
  • Day-to-day living expenses

Every case is assessed on its own circumstances.

What happens if a parent refuses to pay maintenance?

Where a maintenance order exists and payments are not being made, legal enforcement options may be available.

Depending on the circumstances, the court may make orders to recover outstanding amounts or facilitate direct payment arrangements through an employer.

We can advise you on the most appropriate course of action.

Can spousal maintenance continue if my former spouse is working?

Potentially, yes.

The courts consider a range of factors including:

  • Length of the relationship
  • Financial dependency
  • Earning capacity
  • Caregiving responsibilities
  • Overall financial circumstances

Each case is assessed individually.

Can a maintenance order be changed?

Yes.

Where there has been a significant change in financial circumstances, either party may apply to the court to vary an existing maintenance order.

Examples include:

  • Redundancy
  • Illness
  • Business difficulties
  • Increased childcare expenses
  • Changes in income

We can advise on whether a variation application may be appropriate.
