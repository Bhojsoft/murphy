Estate Planning

Securing Your Wealth and Protecting Your Family’s Future

Estate planning is far more than preparing a legal document. It is about ensuring that everything you have built over a lifetime—your property, savings, and business—is protected and passed on smoothly to the people you care about most.

Without proper planning, your estate may face unnecessary delays, tax burdens, and disputes that can place emotional and financial strain on your family.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide clear, structured, and legally robust estate planning solutions designed to protect your legacy and give you complete peace of mind.

The Hidden Risks of an Unprotected Legacy

For many individuals—particularly professionals, business owners, and parents—the future brings understandable concerns:

  • What happens if I lose capacity to manage my affairs?
  • Will my family face large tax bills on inheritance?
  • Could my children be forced to sell assets to cover liabilities?
  • What happens if my estate is frozen after my death?

Without a properly structured estate plan, your assets may be distributed according to default legal rules rather than your personal wishes.

This can result in unnecessary delays, higher tax exposure, and avoidable family conflict.

We help remove uncertainty by creating clear, legally sound plans tailored to your unique circumstances.

The Strategic Advantage: Future Protection with Dignity

Effective estate planning goes beyond what happens after death—it also protects you during your lifetime.

We specialise in integrating long-term safeguards such as:

  • Enduring Powers of Attorney (EPA)
  • Decision Support Service (DSS) compliance
  • Healthcare and financial decision planning

These instruments ensure that if you lose capacity due to illness or injury, trusted individuals can legally manage your affairs without court intervention.

This protects your dignity, autonomy, and financial security.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is estate planning important?

Estate planning ensures your assets are distributed according to your wishes and helps reduce tax exposure, legal delays, and family disputes.

What happens if I die without a will?

If you die without a will, your estate will be distributed according to intestacy rules, which may not reflect your personal wishes.

Can estate planning reduce inheritance tax?

Yes. Proper structuring can help reduce Capital Acquisitions Tax (CAT) liability through reliefs, exemptions, and strategic planning.

What is the difference between a will and an estate plan?

A will is one component of estate planning. A full estate plan may also include trusts, tax planning, business succession strategies, and capacity planning tools.
