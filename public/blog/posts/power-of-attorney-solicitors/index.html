Planning for the future means ensuring that your financial, property, and personal affairs remain protected if you become unable to make decisions for yourself.

A properly drafted Power of Attorney allows you to appoint trusted individuals to act on your behalf, providing certainty for both you and your family during challenging circumstances.

At Martina Murphy Solicitors, we provide practical, compassionate, and legally robust advice on Powers of Attorney, helping clients safeguard their independence, dignity, and assets through effective future planning.

Why Powers of Attorney Matter

Many people assume that close family members automatically have authority to manage their affairs if something unexpected happens.

Unfortunately, this is often not the case.

Clients frequently ask:

  • Who will manage my finances if I become incapacitated?
  • How can my family access important accounts if I cannot act myself?
  • What happens if I lose mental capacity?
  • How can I ensure my healthcare wishes are respected?
  • How can my family avoid lengthy legal proceedings?

Without the appropriate legal arrangements, family members may face delays, court applications, and significant stress during already difficult situations.

A Power of Attorney provides clarity and protection when it is needed most.

Our Power of Attorney Services

We provide comprehensive future-planning solutions tailored to your individual circumstances.

Enduring Power of Attorney (EPA)

An Enduring Power of Attorney allows you to appoint trusted individuals to make decisions on your behalf if you lose the capacity to do so in the future.

An EPA may cover:

  • Financial affairs
  • Property matters
  • Personal welfare decisions
  • Future care planning

Ordinary Powers of Attorney

An Ordinary Power of Attorney can be useful where you need someone to act on your behalf while you remain mentally capable.

These arrangements are commonly used when:

  • Living abroad
  • Travelling extensively
  • Recovering from illness or surgery
  • Managing business affairs remotely

Advance Healthcare Directives

Clear guidance can help reduce uncertainty for loved ones and healthcare professionals.

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I lose capacity without an Enduring Power of Attorney?

Without an EPA, family members may need to apply through formal legal processes before they can manage your affairs or make certain decisions on your behalf.

This can create delays, additional costs, and unnecessary stress during difficult circumstances.

Can I appoint more than one attorney?

Yes.

You can appoint multiple attorneys to act jointly or jointly and severally, depending on your preferences and circumstances.

We can advise on the most suitable structure for your needs.

Does an Ordinary Power of Attorney continue if I lose mental capacity?

No.

An Ordinary Power of Attorney generally ceases to have effect if you lose mental capacity.

An Enduring Power of Attorney is specifically designed to address future incapacity.

Can a Power of Attorney include healthcare decisions?

Yes.

Depending on the arrangements made, Powers of Attorney may work alongside Advance Healthcare Directives to help ensure your wishes are respected if you become unable to communicate them yourself.
