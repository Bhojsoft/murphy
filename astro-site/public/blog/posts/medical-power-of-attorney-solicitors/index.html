Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between a financial and a medical power of attorney?

A financial power of attorney deals with financial matters such as:

  • Property
  • Banking
  • Investments
  • Business affairs

Healthcare decision-making arrangements relate specifically to medical treatment, welfare, and care decisions.

Different people can be appointed for different responsibilities if desired.

Can my appointed representative override my doctor’s advice?

Your representative must act according to your known wishes, values, and preferences while working alongside healthcare professionals involved in your care.

Valid healthcare directives play an important role in guiding those decisions.

When do healthcare decision-making arrangements take effect?

These arrangements generally become relevant only when you lose the capacity to make or communicate specific healthcare decisions yourself.

While you retain capacity, you continue to make your own healthcare decisions.

Do I need a solicitor to prepare these documents?

Professional legal advice helps ensure that your documentation is:

  • Legally valid
  • Properly executed
  • Fully compliant with Irish law
  • Tailored to your circumstances

Careful preparation significantly reduces the risk of future disputes or legal difficulties.
